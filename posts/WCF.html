2023 Western Conference Recap




After watching the first two games of this Western Conference Finals series, it is abundantly clear that the Denver Nuggets are dictating the pace and style of play.


Before diving into the stats and "eye-test," I must express this as a tangent: the media bias against the Denver Nuggets is abhorrent. Maybe because I live in Minneapolis—and root for a small market team—I have this inclination for the underdogs and thus a soft spot for some of these underrepresented metro areas, but wow, is the coverage lopsided. From Twitter to ESPN, the Nuggets are up 2-0 and are not getting their flowers.


In Game 1, the Nuggets showed that they could hang with the Lakers' star-studded roster and even exceed them in offensive prowess, notching a total of 132 points, mainly thanks to their key players. Nikola Jokic had an outstanding game with a triple-double (34 points, 21 rebounds, 14 assists—these are 2k numbers), and Jamal Murray added a substantial 31 points. Their output, coupled with the strong performance from Kentavious Caldwell-Pope and Michael Porter Jr., helped the Nuggets maintain their lead throughout the game. LeBron James and Anthony Davis, despite their combined 66 points, were unable to secure a win. A lack of defense on the Lakers' part was evident, notably from D'Angelo Russell's +/- rating of -25.


For the Lakers, one positive aspect was the strong performance of Rui Hachimura, whose +/- rating was 10 - the highest on the team. Rui's play, LeBron James and Anthony Davis's consistent efforts kept the game competitive. Still, the Lakers' overall defensive shortcomings allowed the Nuggets to maintain control of the game.


In Game 2, the Nuggets built on their previous win despite a less efficient offensive performance. This time, the spotlight was on Jamal Murray, who scored 23 of his 37 points in the fourth quarter, leading the Nuggets to overcome a deficit and secure a 108-103 win. Even though Jokic did not match his Game 1 performance, his solid double-double (23 points, 17 rebounds) and the team's overall defensive efforts were enough to secure the win.


Anthony Davis struggled offensively for the Lakers, shooting only 26.7% from the field. At the same time, LeBron James again performed strongly with 22 points, 9 rebounds, and 10 assists. Many people lamented LeBron's lackluster three-point shooting this game. Still, Austin Reaves kept them in this game outside of LeBron. The lack of secondary scoring and support from other Lakers players was again an apparent problem, particularly considering Davis's offensive struggles.


Looking ahead, the Lakers will need to address their defensive issues and find more scoring options beyond James and Davis if they want to avoid falling into a 3-0 series hole. The Nuggets, meanwhile, will aim to keep exploiting the Lakers' weaknesses, with Jokic and Murray continuing to lead the way. Their performance in Game 3 will be critical in determining the series' trajectory. If Denver can take a commanding 3-0 lead, it will be tough, impossible even, for the Lakers to mount a comeback.


Me, personally? I think the data from this postseason would suggest that the Nuggets would fight up to a Game 7. However, my intuition and that "eye-test" from watching these games tell me Nuggets in 5, maybe 6, but that's being extremely generous to the Lakers. I have yet to have the time to aggregate any CSVs, scrape any NBA stat sites, and take them to Python, but I did take team stats, individual splits, and articles following each of the two games and feed them to ChatGPT. OpenAI says Nuggets in 7. So...
